## 5. KEY METRICS VCS OBSESS OVER

### A. Growth Metrics

**1. ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**
- **Target:** 200%+ YoY in early years, 100%+ at scale
- **Why it matters:** Core indicator of business momentum

**2. MoM Growth Rate**
- **Target:** 15-20% monthly in Year 1-2
- **Why it matters:** Shows compounding traction

**3. Gross Dollar Retention (GDR)**
- **Target:** >90%
- **Formula:** (Starting ARR - Churned ARR) / Starting ARR
- **Why it matters:** Measures core product value

**4. Net Dollar Retention (NDR)**
- **Target:** 110-130%
- **Formula:** (Starting ARR - Churn + Expansion) / Starting ARR
- **Why it matters:** Shows expansion potential; >120% = elite

**5. Magic Number**
- **Target:** >0.75 (good), >1.0 (great)
- **Formula:** (Net New ARR This Quarter) / (Sales & Marketing Spend Last Quarter)
- **Why it matters:** Sales efficiency; how quickly you can grow

---

### B. Unit Economics

**1. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**
- **Target:** <$500 at scale for SMB, <$5K for enterprise
- **Formula:** Total Sales & Marketing Spend / New Customers
- **Track by:** Channel, cohort, product tier

**2. Lifetime Value (LTV)**
- **Target:** 3x CAC minimum, 5x+ ideal
- **Formula:** ARPU √ó Gross Margin √ó (1 / Churn Rate)
- **Why it matters:** Shows long-term profitability

**3. LTV/CAC Ratio**
- **Target:** 3x minimum, 5x+ ideal, 7x+ elite
- **Why it matters:** Core metric for venture-scale business

**4. CAC Payback Period**
- **Target:** <12 months (good), <6 months (great)
- **Formula:** CAC / (ARPU √ó Gross Margin)
- **Why it matters:** Capital efficiency, burn rate

**5. Gross Margin**
- **Target:** 70-80%+ for SaaS
- **Formula:** (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
- **Why it matters:** Scalability potential

---

### C. Engagement & Retention

**1. Monthly Active Users (MAU)**
- **Target:** >60% of registered users
- **Why it matters:** Product stickiness

**2. Daily Active Users / MAU Ratio**
- **Target:** >20% (good), >40% (exceptional)
- **Why it matters:** Habit formation

**3. Churn Rate**
- **Target:** <5% monthly (SMB), <2% (enterprise)
- **Formula:** Customers Lost / Starting Customers
- **Track:** Logo churn AND dollar churn

**4. NPS (Net Promoter Score)**
- **Target:** >40 (good), >50 (excellent)
- **Why it matters:** Product-market fit signal

---

### D. Sales Efficiency

**1. Win Rate**
- **Target:** >20% (cold), >50% (qualified leads)
- **Track by:** Lead source, sales rep, product tier

**2. Average Sales Cycle**
- **Target:** <30 days (SMB), <90 days (enterprise)
- **Why it matters:** Capital efficiency

**3. Average Contract Value (ACV)**
- **Target:** >$1K for VC-backable SaaS
- **Track trend:** Should increase over time

---

### E. The Rule of 40

**Formula:** Revenue Growth Rate (%) + Profit Margin (%)

**Targets:**
- >40 = Good
- >50 = Very Good  
- >60 = Exceptional

**Example (Year 4):**
- Revenue Growth: 205%
- EBITDA Margin: -15%
- Rule of 40: 190 (Exceptional despite losses)

**Why it matters:** Balances growth vs profitability; VCs use this to compare companies
